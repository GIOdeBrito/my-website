About me

Ever since I was a little boy, technology has always fascinated me, from simple remotely controlled toy cars, to more complex machines such as gaming consoles and computers.

Today I am a self-learned web and desktop developer, studying and practicing, either for fun or for learning, basically non-stop since my 16 years old. Web was not always my primary focus though; it came much later, as I began my journey in programming writing code in the Unity3D engine, using MonoDelevop as IDE back when the Boo language was still available, with the now defunct UnityScript — a peculiar but also rather curious kind of JavaScript for game development, it was, indeed, a form of typed JS, way before Microsoft coined the well-known TypeScript.

When I am not programming I enjoy reading history and fantasy books. Working on other side personal projects, such as translating and lettering books and comics, editing videos for me or my friends, researching about ancient dead languages of old, et cetera. At times, I am also keen to trying new cooking recipes when I am in the mood to get my hands dirty in the kitchen.

I am very fond of videogames as they have been a big part of my life ever since — although I do not play as much in the current past years — and I had my first contact with the English language through story-driven RPGs, and gaming was one of the key factors to motivate me to learn it. My favorite games include: Demon's Souls, Final Fantasy X, God Hand, Fallout New Vegas, The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim and DOOM 2016 are a few.
